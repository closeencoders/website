Getting Into Go as a Noob

2026-03-17

Trying to move from Java to Go has been relatively easy so far, but to really get into the nitty gritty, I need good tutorials and resources.

The internet obviously has a plethora of resources, but which ones are good, and which will lead me astray?

Starting With the Source

Go's website itself has some amazing resources in /learn, in the same way many other modern languages and open source tools do.

Before you go buy a book or something, and if you already know how to code, even just a bit, I highly recommend working through "Go by Example" before doing anything else. It is super easy and touches on just about everything you need to get off to a good start.

Then go build a few super simple projects. You know, your typical TODO app, etc.

Influencers

If you are anything like me, you learn by doing and by watching others who are more skilled than you do their thing. Over time, I have found a handful of YouTubers, some of whom also have podcasts, who use Go regularly and have a much higher level of skill than I do.

Just about every time I watch their higher quality content, I learn something or get a different perspective on how to approach development. I will keep this simple and just leave a list here with a brief description.

  • Mongoose Studios - No AI, no BS streams about his adventures developing Go applications.
  • Avery - I talked about this guy in a previous post. He has a great introduction playlist to Raylib and Go if you are just starting out like me.
  • Melkey - An ex Twitch engineer who often uses Go and makes tutorials, and talks about the industry as a whole.
  • ThePrimeagen - An ex Netflix engineer with a larger channel. He often talks about and uses Go for projects.
  • Will update this list as I go...

Tools and Misc

There are so many free resources and tools out there. I also do not condone pirating books as PDFs, but there are several blatantly free Go books. Just take a look at Book Search on Goodreads to get a feel for what people like and what works.

Another great resource I see so many people reference is roadmap.sh. There is a section called "Go Roadmap" if you scroll down a bit. If you really need a structured approach to learning just about anything in tech, it is a great place to start.
