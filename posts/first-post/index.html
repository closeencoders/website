I used to be a Java developer.

I woke up the other day and had an almost comical midlife crisis, rather cliché when I think about it now.

I don't want my tombstone to say: "He developed a great card processing application, and the product team was proud of him until he was laid off and it was all replaced with AI."

But guess what, sometimes you don't get a choice. Most people have many dire responsibilities and debt, there is NO option.

For a long time, I was in a similar position. There really was no choice. I got out of bed around 5 a.m. and worked 10+ hours a day while studying for certificates and taking courses at night, often deep into the weekend, for almost 10 years.

The catch is, I saved and invested as much as humanly possible until I finally reached a threshold where I feel comfortable taking a risk and moving into something else. I am NOT above working. I just want to be an entrepreneur, not knee deep in a pile of toxic Jira tickets and a half a$$ed Confluence page from 2017.

I feel this is my one and only chance in this life.

I doubt anyone, human at least, will ever read this, but I cannot express enough, if you are fortunate, the importance of starting your financial journey ASAP.

It is, unfortunately, one of the only true ways to generate options for yourself. No get rich quick BS. Just try your best to stack bread, put a portion of it into index funds, or whatever beats inflation to your liking, and wait 10+ years. If you are lucky, options will start to appear if you are prepared for them.

If you get an opportunity, just build and do what you want while you still can.
